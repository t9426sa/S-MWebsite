ROCK VALLEY STUDENT:

JOHN MAYES

John's Experience


John is an engineering student at Rock Valley College. He started his Associate in Science degree in 2019. He intends to transfer to Iowa State University. He is very impressed with the quality of engineering classes, with the quality of computer lab, and with all machines, 3-D printers and machinery related with engineering. Visit our Image Gallery in this page.

Rock Valley offers Engineering Associates and Bachelors degrees, with a partnership with NYU. However, if you intend to continue in another University, don't worry: The Advising & Transfer Counseling Center could give you the suport you need.

"I am impressed with idea that we could learn and practice in real machines, as we are at a shop", said John. And some teachers are concerned in teaching and helping the society. Check the work of professor Clark and the project Enabling The Future, which John made a Prosthetic Hand for a child. Studying at Rock Valley means be Social Involved!

Health, Science, and Sustainability

Health Science Center


At first look at this state-of-the-art building is that we are not in a campus. Its aesthetics really brings us the peace and the calm that a hospital should be.

And its amazing 79,000 square foot is so innovative that is within the Sustainability, to get the Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) Silver certification. Sustainable efforts for the building will include:

  • 50/50 Geo-thermal field, 76 bores, 350’ deep;
  • Chill-beams;
  • Daylighting;
  • Energy Recovery Wheels in air handling units;

Math, Science, and Respect

Karl J. Jacobs Center for Science and Math

Being actual with the global awareness of sustainability, in 2011 this building was created with wide innovative sustainable technologies.

Besides the respect with nature, which we can check in our Galery, Rock Valley College made a tribute for Dr. Karl J. Jacobs. He served as the president of Rock Valley College from 1969 – 1997 and he was highly respected by local leaders.

Among the isles and classrooms, this 106,000 square foot building has spaces dedicated for students. The Student Life Spaces offers cosy sofas and confort, as sometimes, students are far from home for many hours.
